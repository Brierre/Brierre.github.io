What's new? May 10, 2024

Music teachers are always macro-planning far in advance, while also micro-planning what is in the now. As the school year winds down, I find myself trying to think ahead to my summer learning as I write program notes and put the finishing touches on the concert(s) at hand. Musical season has ended and our run was well done, despite a really long and grueling prep. We are heading to the local theatre awards next week, and then I am finished with that chapter of my life for now.

On the home front, things are exciting. I have not one, but two graduating seniors this year. My older son is finishing up his coursework for a degree in Comp Sci from Lock Haven University, and my younger son is about to graduate from high school. I hope to be able to create web applications with my older son in the future, as his knowledge of programming fills in the gaps in my knowledge. Over the last four years, we have helped each other get past the imposter syndrome that comes along with learning to develop programs. I look forward to being adults together with these two. My younger son wants to attend college closer to his dad and that fills me with a little bit of mom-dread, thinking that he won't be right here with me for the foreseeable future, but he and his dad are close, and I can't be happier that they will get to spend more time together.

As for programming, my current project is to satisfy a couple of goals. I haven't written any new apps in several months, since the school year began. In order to get back in the swing of things, and in order to satisfy a requirement for a jazz graduate course I am taking, I will be creating an application similar to the final projects for boot camp using React and Vite. I have begun the process of coding during my lunches, but I can't wait to get home where I have no limitations on the network that do not allow me to see my work. I'll be figuring out how to host the project this time, bypassing mock apis.

February 24, 2024

Things are definitely crazy in the life of this choir director/musical director/marching band director/mom. I keep thinking (or maybe it's just hoping) that there will be a break at some point some day. Right now, I'm three weeks out from our musical, which is Damn Yankees this year. I may have bitten off more than I can chew with this one, but what's a little sheer panic among friends, right?

Software development just has to wait when every spare moment is eaten up by sewing twelve pairs of vintage-style baseball pants and creating 24 baseball jerseys with a Cricut and a heat press--and that's just the costuming part of things. I know these kids and I know myself and my stage director, Michele (who is awesome!) and I'm positive we will pull this off and it will be great, but the sheer panic thing is real!

I have been thinking about developing lately, however, and my plans are to finish a C++ course that I purchased several years ago, and put aside because I felt a little overwhelmed by pointers. I purchased a refurbished macbook a couple of weeks ago, so I can see what the buzz is all about with those. I'm ready for the break in musical activities--you know, those two days in between when musical season ends and marching band season starts once again--so I can get some things set up and get working on that class. I also plan to brush up on my boot camp stuff since I feel like I forget everything already. And finally, I want to get this website moving along a little bit. It has been stagnant, and though it is just a template-based site, I really hate the idea of having lorem ipsum on my pages. I didn't come up with that myself. :D

July 10, 2023

I'm now about 2/3 of the way through Promineo Tech Front End Software Development Boot Camp, and I am astonished at how much I've learned. I find myself immensely enjoying putting together websites using new (to me) technologies and skills. Right now, we are getting into React, which is something we will be able to use in the real world. It's been around awhile, but it isn't imminently going out of date like some of the other tools we have been using to learn the ropes.

We are going into the final project season for class and it is going to be tough. I'm excited to work on my project--it's either going to be a Warhammer 40k Army Builder or a Disc Golf Scorekeeping and Course Notes app. However, it is due the week after band camp, and as you may have seen from another part of my page, I am one of our band directors, and I also write the show. I am on the hook to write the drill and the band front routine this week and have to somehow squeeze in coding homework around that. Then I have to teach it during long days at band camp. I hate to be late, so I will probably find a way to get my homework done on time somehow, I just haven't figured out how that will be yet. Divine intervention may be needed.

April 8, 2023

I spent this week taking short classes online in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to prepare for my Front End Developer Boot Camp through Promineo Tech and Gateway Community College in Arizona. I'll be studying several weeks concentrating on each language, but I had the time to take these mini courses through a site called GreatLearning, and they've been quite helpful. I'm looking forward to writing code in the front end program. After having finished their Back End Boot Camp, I was impressed by the community at Promineo Tech and wanted to go full stack.

My first boot camp was also through Promineo Tech, only the school was the Community College of Philadelphia, and it is pretty incredible just how much information I crammed into 18 weeks. I went from having faked my way through some Lua code a couple of years ago (written for a private FFXI open-source project) and having no idea what I was doing, to writing an entire REST application from scratch, using our homework and several other students' projects as models. I chose Gateway Community College this time around because 1.) I LOVE the desert. It's my happy place, and 2.) The only time I can take a class on Thursdays this semester is after 9pm, so the PST of Arizona this time of year worked out perfectly. I'm one of those people who believes in signs and to some extent, destiny. Arizona is definitely calling my name here!

When I began the back end boot camp, I had no intentions of looking for work in the field of software development, I simply wanted to learn to do a new skill the right way. Now that I have been able to create things and have seen the fruits of my labor, I am not entirely ruling out a career change. My current job is teaching choir, musical theatre, and general music in grades 6-12. I love my students and I love teaching music, so I'm not ready to jump in with both feet yet.
